How to choose exercises based on your goals

March 3, 2026 · 6 min read

With hundreds of weight training exercises out there, it is easy to feel overwhelmed. Should I do squats or leg press? Bench press or flyes? The answer depends entirely on your goals and your context.

Exercise categories

Before choosing, you need to understand the two main families:

  • Compound exercises: they engage multiple joints and muscle groups at once. Examples: squat, bench press, deadlift, pull-ups, rows.
  • Isolation exercises: they target a single muscle group. Examples: bicep curls, lateral raises, leg extensions, cable flyes.

A good program combines both, but in different proportions depending on the goal.

Goal: build strength

Strength is primarily developed with heavy loads on compound movements. These exercises recruit the maximum number of muscle fibers.

Priority exercises:

  • Squat: the king of lower body exercises
  • Bench press: the benchmark for upper body strength
  • Deadlift: works the entire posterior chain
  • Overhead press: shoulder strength and stability

Parameters: 3-6 reps, 4-6 sets, 3-5 minutes rest, loads at 80-90% of 1RM.

Goal: hypertrophy (muscle growth)

To maximize muscle growth, training volume (sets × reps × load) is the key factor. You combine compound and isolation exercises to target each muscle from different angles.

Sample chest session structure:

  • Bench press: 4x8-10 (strength and overall mass)
  • Incline dumbbell press: 3x10-12 (upper chest)
  • Cable flyes: 3x12-15 (stretch and contraction)
  • Dips: 3x8-12 (lower chest + triceps)

Parameters: 8-12 reps, 3-4 sets, 60-90 seconds rest, loads at 65-80% of 1RM.

Goal: muscular endurance

Muscular endurance is the ability to repeat an effort over time. Useful for combat sports, running, or simply avoiding fatigue in daily life.

Recommended exercises:

  • Circuit training: chain 5-8 exercises with minimal rest
  • Bodyweight exercises: push-ups, squats, burpees, mountain climbers
  • Supersets: chain two antagonist exercises without rest

Parameters: 15-25 reps, 2-3 sets, 30-45 seconds rest, light loads at 50-65% of 1RM.

Goal: weight loss

Contrary to popular belief, weight training is an excellent ally for losing fat. Muscle at rest burns more calories than fat, and the “afterburn” effect (EPOC) after an intense session increases your energy expenditure for hours.

Optimal strategy:

  • Keep heavy compound exercises to maintain muscle mass in a caloric deficit
  • Add HIIT cardio (high-intensity intervals) 2-3 times per week
  • Maintain sufficient training volume: reduce volume but not intensity

Adapting exercises to your equipment

Not all trainees have access to a full gym. Here is how to adapt:

Full gym

Take advantage of everything: barbells, machines, cables, dumbbells. Prioritize free weights for main movements and machines for isolation work.

Home gym (dumbbells + bench)

Focus on dumbbells: dumbbell bench press, single-arm rows, goblet squats, dumbbell shoulder press, lunges. Dumbbells offer freedom of movement that compensates for the absence of machines.

Bodyweight only

Push-ups (and variations), dips, pull-ups, pistol squats, handstand push-ups. Progression comes from increasing the difficulty of the variation rather than adding weight.
