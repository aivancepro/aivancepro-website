Fundamentals: Understanding Strength Training

Strength training aims to build muscles using resistance (weights, bands, bodyweight). Cardio targets cardiovascular endurance. Both are complementary and AIVancePro helps you combine them based on your goals.
Strength training strengthens bones, improves metabolism, reduces injury risk, improves posture and contributes to mental health. It is recommended at all ages.
Hypertrophy targets muscle size (8-12 reps), maximal strength works with heavy loads (1-5 reps), and muscular endurance uses lighter loads with many repetitions (15+). AIVancePro adjusts the parameters based on your goal.
Generally 3 to 5 times per week for optimal results. The AI coach adjusts frequency and volume based on your availability and recovery.
Progressive overload means gradually increasing weight, reps or volume to keep making progress. It is the fundamental principle of progression in strength training. AIVancePro tracks your progress and helps you apply this principle.
Yes, strength training increases your basal metabolic rate and burns calories during and after your workout. Combined with proper nutrition, it is one of the most effective ways to lose fat.
Not necessarily. Consistency, proper technique and progressive overload matter more than absolute weight. Even with moderate loads, you can achieve excellent results.
No equipment is required! You can start with bodyweight exercises. To progress faster, a pair of adjustable dumbbells is a great investment. AIVancePro adapts to whatever equipment you have.
A balanced program should cover all major groups: chest, back, shoulders, arms, legs and abs. The AI coach ensures balanced muscle distribution across your sessions.
Muscles repair and grow stronger during rest. Without adequate recovery, you risk overtraining and injury. AIVancePro analyzes your workload and alerts you if your volume is too high.
Yes, as long as you maintain proper technique and progress gradually. AIVancePro offers beginner-friendly exercises with detailed instructions to minimize risk.

A mix of weight training (to preserve muscle mass) and cardio/HIIT (to increase calorie burn) is ideal. The AI coach creates balanced programs for this goal.

During a cut, maintaining heavy weights with moderate reps (6-12) helps preserve muscle mass. Increasing reps with lighter weights can add a caloric effect, but should not replace heavy work.

Staying Healthy & Fit

2 to 3 weight training sessions per week combined with some cardio is enough to maintain good health. The AI coach creates balanced programs tailored to this goal.
Absolutely! Strength training is actually recommended after 40 to fight age-related muscle loss. The AI coach adapts intensity and volume to your profile.

Losing Weight

Yes, strength training increases your basal metabolic rate, meaning you burn more calories even at rest. Combined with a calorie deficit, it is very effective.
A 45 to 60-minute weight training session burns an average of 200 to 400 calories, depending on intensity. The “afterburn” effect means you continue burning calories for hours after your session.
