Beginner workout program: where to start

February 7, 2026 · 7 min read

You’ve decided to start weight training? Great decision for your health! It’s one of the best choices you can make for both your body and mind. But with so much information out there, it’s easy to feel overwhelmed. This guide gives you the basics to get started effectively.

Why start weight training?

Weight training isn’t just about lifting weights to build muscle. Its benefits go far beyond aesthetics:

  • Bone health: strengthens bones and helps prevent osteoporosis
  • Metabolism: increases your resting calorie expenditure
  • Posture: corrects muscular imbalances
  • Mental health: reduces stress and boosts self-confidence
  • Longevity: muscle mass is one of the best predictors of a longer life

Define your goals

Before you touch a barbell, ask yourself: why? Your goal will determine your approach:

  • Muscle gain: focus on compound exercises, heavy loads and a caloric surplus
  • Weight loss: combine weight training with cardio and a moderate caloric deficit
  • General fitness: balanced program with gradual progression
  • Pure strength: low reps, maximum loads and longer rest periods

The 5 fundamental exercises

As a beginner, focus on compound movements that work multiple muscle groups at the same time:

1. The squat

The king of all exercises. It targets the quadriceps, glutes, hamstrings and core. Start with bodyweight or an empty barbell to master the technique before adding weight.

2. The bench press

The go-to exercise for chest, shoulders and triceps. Keep your shoulder blades squeezed together and your feet firmly planted on the floor.

3. The deadlift

Excellent for the back, glutes and the entire posterior chain. Technique is essential: keep your back straight and drive with your legs.

4. The row

Essential for a balanced back. Barbell or dumbbell rows strengthen the lats, rhomboids and biceps.

5. The overhead press

For strong shoulders and a balanced upper body. Standing or seated, this is a fundamental movement you should not skip.

Beginner program 3 days/week

Here is a simple and effective full body program to get you started. Alternate between Day A and Day B with at least one rest day between each session.

Day A – Full Body

  • Squat: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Bench press: 3 x 8-10
  • Barbell row: 3 x 8-10
  • Overhead press: 3 x 8-10
  • Plank: 3 x 30 seconds

Day B – Full Body

  • Romanian deadlift: 3 x 8-10
  • Incline dumbbell press: 3 x 10-12
  • Pull-ups (or lat pulldown): 3 x 8-10
  • Lateral raises: 3 x 12-15
  • Bicep curls: 3 x 10-12

Progressive overload: the key to success

The most important principle in weight training is progressive overload. Each week, try to slightly increase the weight (1–2 kg), the number of reps or the number of sets. Without progression, there are no results.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Going too heavy too fast: technique first, heavy weights later
  • Neglecting recovery: muscles are built during rest, not during training
  • Changing program every week: stick with a program for at least 8 weeks
  • Skipping legs: a classic mistake that creates dangerous imbalances
  • Comparing yourself to others: everyone progresses at their own pace
