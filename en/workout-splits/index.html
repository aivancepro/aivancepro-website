Workout Splits: Which Training Program Should You Choose?

Full Body, Upper/Lower, PPL or Bro Split? Complete comparison to find the ideal split based on your level and availability.

What is a workout split?

A workout split is how you distribute your muscle groups across your training days. Choosing the right split depends on your experience, goals and the number of days you can dedicate to the gym.

Workout Split Overview Photo

Full Body

3 days / week

Each session works the entire body. Ideal for beginners and those with limited time. Each muscle is stimulated 3x per week, maximizing protein synthesis for novices.

Best for: Beginners, busy schedules, returning after a break.

Mon: Full Body AWed: Full Body BFri: Full Body C

Upper / Lower

4 days / week

Alternates between upper body and lower body. Good balance between volume and recovery. Each muscle is trained 2x per week.

Best for: Intermediates, steady and structured progression.

Mon: UpperTue: LowerThu: UpperFri: Lower

Push / Pull / Legs (PPL)

6 days / week

The most popular split in strength training. Separates pushing movements, pulling movements and legs. High volume with 2x frequency per muscle.

Best for: Intermediate to advanced, dedicated lifters who can train 6 days.

Mon: PushTue: PullWed: LegsThu: PushFri: PullSat: Legs

Bro Split

5 days / week

One muscle group per day. High volume per muscle but low frequency (1x/week). Popular among advanced bodybuilders.

Best for: Advanced lifters seeking maximum volume per muscle group.

Mon: ChestTue: BackWed: ShouldersThu: LegsFri: Arms

How to choose your split?

The best split is the one you can follow consistently. Consistency always beats theoretical optimization. Ask yourself these questions:

  • How many days per week can you train?
  • How long have you been strength training?
  • What is your main goal (strength, hypertrophy, general health)?
