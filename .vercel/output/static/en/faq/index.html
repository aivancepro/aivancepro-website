Fundamentals: understanding weight training

Weight training aims to strengthen muscles using resistance (weights, bands, bodyweight). Cardio targets cardiovascular endurance. Both are complementary and AIVancePro helps you combine them based on your goals.
Weight training strengthens bones, improves metabolism, reduces injury risk, improves posture, and contributes to mental health. It is recommended at all ages.
Hypertrophy targets muscle volume (8-12 reps), maximal strength works with heavy loads (1-5 reps), and muscular endurance uses light loads with many reps (15+). AIVancePro adjusts parameters based on your goal.
Generally 3 to 5 times per week for optimal results. The AI coach adjusts frequency and volume based on your availability and recovery.
Progressive overload means gradually increasing load, reps, or volume to keep progressing. It's the fundamental principle of progression in weight training. AIVancePro tracks your progress and helps you apply this principle.
Yes, weight training increases your basal metabolism and burns calories during and after training. Combined with proper nutrition, it's one of the most effective ways to lose fat.
Not necessarily. Consistency, proper technique, and progressive overload matter more than absolute load. Even with moderate weights, you can achieve excellent results.
No equipment is required! You can start with bodyweight. To progress faster, a pair of adjustable dumbbells is a great investment. AIVancePro adapts to your equipment.
A balanced program should cover all major groups: chest, back, shoulders, arms, legs, and abs. The AI coach ensures balanced muscle distribution in your sessions.
Muscles repair and strengthen during rest. Without sufficient recovery, you risk overtraining and injuries. AIVancePro analyzes your load and alerts you if your volume is too high.
Yes, as long as you follow proper technique and progress gradually. AIVancePro offers beginner-friendly exercises with detailed instructions to minimize risks.

Fundamentals by goal

Cutting & getting lean

A mix of weight training (to preserve muscle mass) and cardio/HIIT (to increase caloric expenditure) is ideal. The AI coach creates balanced programs for this goal.
Cardio helps create a caloric deficit, but it's not mandatory. Nutrition plays a more important role. Cardio is an additional tool the AI coach can integrate into your program.
During a cut, maintaining heavy loads with moderate reps (6-12) helps preserve muscle mass. Increasing reps with light loads can add a caloric effect, but shouldn't replace heavy work.
Yes, high-intensity bodyweight exercises (burpees, push-ups, jump squats...) are very effective for burning calories. AIVancePro can generate HIIT circuits with no equipment.

Building muscle & getting stronger

The AI generates hypertrophy programs with the right volume, intensity, and frequency. It tracks your progress and adjusts loads to ensure optimal progressive overload.
It depends on your availability. Push/Pull/Legs, Upper/Lower, or Full Body are all effective. The AI coach recommends the optimal split based on your training days.
Initial strength gains are felt within 2-3 weeks. Visible physical changes typically appear after 6 to 8 weeks of regular training. AIVancePro helps you visualize this progress.
No, supplements are not necessary. A balanced diet rich in protein is sufficient. Creatine and whey can be convenient but are not essential.

Staying fit & healthy

2 to 3 weight training sessions per week combined with some cardio are enough to maintain good health. The AI coach creates balanced programs adapted to this goal.
Absolutely! Weight training is even recommended after 40 to combat age-related muscle loss. The AI coach adapts intensity and volume to your profile.
No, rest is essential for progress. 3 to 5 sessions per week with rest days is enough. AIVancePro integrates recovery into the planning.
Yes, the AI coach can generate programs focused on functional strength with compound exercises and movements that improve everyday activities.

Losing weight

Yes, weight training increases basal metabolism, meaning you burn more calories even at rest. Combined with a caloric deficit, it's very effective.
A 45 to 60-minute weight training session burns an average of 200 to 400 calories, depending on intensity. The "afterburn" effect means you continue burning calories for hours after the session.
Yes, a caloric deficit is necessary for weight loss. Weight training helps maintain muscle mass during weight loss to avoid the "skinny fat" effect.
Both are complementary. Weight training preserves muscle and increases metabolism, cardio burns extra calories. AIVancePro can combine both in your programs.
